Through Post-Atomic Eyes

Poster: main page

I felt that the first frame should represent what a storybook is and what the rest of the pages entail. Inspired and rejuvenated from my recent holiday trip to Montreal, I decided to create a storybook capturing the essence of this memorable experience. The first frame depicts the journey from Toronto to Montreal, utilizing three triangle shapes at the top with keyframes to create a fadeIn effect, symbolizing the transition.

Montreal on Wheels: Exploring the City by Car

While driving, I recalled a moment of confusion encountering road signs in French, and I decided to incorporate that memory into the storybook. On the next page, I aimed to capture the feeling of arriving somewhere, symbolized by a Stop Sign with the French word 'ARRÊT,' indicating a momentary pause during the journey. To convey a dynamic sense of movement, I animated the car with a subtle up-and-down motion. Clicking on the text 'Drive In Montreal' smoothly transitions to the next page, utilizing the fade-in effect introduced in the first frame to signify moving to the next scene.

Montreal on Wheels: Exploring the City by Car

If you drive towards the Old Port area in Montreal, you'll come across La Grande Roue de Montréal, a symbolic Ferris wheel and a popular photo spot for many tourists. It signifies my road trip from Toronto to Montreal and the arrival at this iconic location. I focused on replicating the Ferris wheel, implementing rotations of objects to simulate its movement. To return to the beginning, simply press the text with the Fade-in effect, acting as a button for navigation.
